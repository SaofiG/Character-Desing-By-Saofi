Characters on fictional stories

At the moment to create a story, there's nothing more important than the characters, their history, looks, and development.

A little about me

Character physical appearance

Most of the characters on animated series are used to have only one or maybe two outfits, that's typically a resource used by the creators to save money on designs, it also can make easier the job of the animators, still, some characters appear with new designs every episode, a couple of examples would be Star Butterfly, from “Star vs The Forces of Evil” and Connie, from “Steven Universe”.



With characters that only have one outfit, a change in their physical appearance can be related to their development in the story, like a change in their personality or point of view of their world, an example of these would be Isabella Madrigal, from the recent Disney film “Encanto”; a change on the character's clothing can also be a one-episode thing, for a good example we can talk about “Miraculous: Tales of Ladybug and Chat Noir”, on this show, there have been episodes where the characters wear new outfits or accessories for specific situations.



The changes can also be attributed to time, after time in the story's universe passes and the characters grow up it is possible that they can receive changes in their clothes, look or even personalities, some examples are:

  • Sasuke Uchiha, from the “Naruto” series, who between “Naruto” and “Naruto Shippuden” suffered changes in his clothing and personality.

  • Kipo, from “Kipo And The Age Of Wonderbeast”, at the finale of the series years had passed, and the main characters had changed their looks.

  • Claire Nuñes, from the “Tales of Arcadia” series, after the ending of “Trollhunters” Claire didn't reappear until the beginning of “Wizards” where she had several changes on her design.

Character Development

Relatable character

The character's physical appearance is what usually calls the audience's attention, still, their personalities is what makes them more likable or interesting; a character with a sweet and kind personality is expected to have more attention from the public, but this ain't always the case, people are more likely to prefer the "mean" characters, or those that are kinda sassy or funny.

A character does not only has to be striking, but it also needs to have good development to be a good character, it has to be relatable for the viewer, the characters may be fictional, still, these can go through the same stuff real people do, the more realistic a character is the more relatable it becomes, and it could even turn into a community's comfort character, let's talk about a few pretty interesting characters:

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Mason "Dipper" Pines, from "Gravity Falls", a young boy who lives a crazy summer with his grunkle Stan, his twin sister Mabel and some other friends the twins made during the story; Dipper is a 12-13 year old boy with self-confidence issues, like a lot kids are at that age; he's started being insecure about his masculinity, strength, the way he looked and some other things not many people give importance, like his birthmark or his name; during the show, Dipper grows more confident, overcoming his insecurities and fears, after having to save his friends, family, and even the whole town from all kinds of weird crautures, after all the adventures, he never stopped being that smart little guy from the beginning.

Dipper is also a big source of theories for the fandom, well based on his attitude and some other hints that can be found on the show and the official book, the fans have theorized that Dipper may be a transgender boy, and for some people from the trans community Dipper has become a comfort character that they can relate to.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Mabel Pines is Dipper's older twin sister, she's a very hyperactive child, who's always messing around and having fun, she always seems to be happy, and she's got a smile on her face most of the time, almost the opposite of her brother whose pretty serious most of the time, because of these differences, the twins are used to have fights, well Mabel's childish behavior makes her seem immature, but the reality behind her behavior, is fear, fear of growing up and having to leave a lot behind, Mabel is scared that growing up could mean getting apart from her friends and family, during the last episodes of the show, everything goes crazy, Bill, the villain, has apparently won the battle, Mabel and Dipper are speared, Dipper wanted to stay in Gravity Falls and study with his grunkle Ford, but Mabel wasn't okay with that, they got into a fight and Bill used that to trick Mabel, causing the “weirdomagedon” and trapping Mabel inside some kind of magic bubble prison where she could be “happy”, everything inside the bubble was created based on Mabel's fantasies, all joy, wonder and cats, she was the ruler of that “magical place” where she could have everything she wanted, even a “cooler” version of her brother, still, none of it was real, and Mabel accepted it at the end, she had to overcome her fears to be able to help her friends to save the town, at the end the twins had learned that the most important thing was being together to support each other, and they come back home expecting the next summer.

Just as Mabel, a lot of pre-teens have been trough a lot of pressure from their families and society, well adults always seem to expect them to be mature, there always seems to be that teenagers are expected to act like adults while being treated like kids, Mabel tries to ignore the expectations and live her life with all fun and games, so she won't feel like she's loosing her childhood, and that's what many kids do to handle the pressure, others are more likely to opt for another option, obedience, like Pacifica Northwest, another character from the show, a girl from a rich family that wasn't allowed to act like a child, she couldn't wear what she wanted, she couldn't choose who to be friends with, she had to be “perfect”, she was so terrified of her parents punishments that with just ringing a bell they had total control over her, as if she were a trained dog, these change when the Pines twins become her friends, and Pacifica finally takes a decision of her own, doing the right thing, even though her parents got mad, her new friends were proud of her, and so was herself.
